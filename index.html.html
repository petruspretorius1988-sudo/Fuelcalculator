How Jumping Works in EVE Frontier (click to expand)

Your single jump range depends on three main things: your ship, the heat in the system, and how heavy your ship is right now.

  • Ship Type: Every ship has a different “heat tolerance” and base mass. Some ships (like Carom or Stride) handle heat well and can jump farther in warmer systems. Heavier ships need more “cooling room” to jump the same distance.
  • System Heat (Temperature): Systems near stars are hot. The hotter the system, the less distance you can jump in one go. If the temperature reaches 90 or higher, you cannot jump at all using fuel (you are in a “heat trap”). Cool outskirts (usually below 10–20) give you the longest possible jumps.
  • Fuel & Ship Weight (Mass): Fuel itself adds weight. The heavier your ship (hull + cargo + fuel), the shorter each jump becomes. Fuel also limits your total travel distance across many jumps — better fuel quality lets you go farther on the same tank.
  • Adaptive Skill: This skill improves how well your ship handles heat, giving you extra jump range (2% bonus per skill level).

Quick tip: For the longest jumps, stay in cool areas (low temperature), fly as light as possible, and train your Adaptive skill.
